2022 Apr - 2022 June
VR Driving School

A Hong Kong–oriented VR driving-school prototype in Unity: a drivable map based on the Ho Man Tin test route, exam-style rules, and lightweight pedestrian and traffic AI so practice feels closer to real constraints. The experience was designed for learner drivers and stakeholders to evaluate training scenarios in head-mounted VR before hardware and tracking were scaled up for the later Smart Traffic Fund programme.

Integrated eye tracking for immersion and for studying where drivers look under test conditions—directly related to the HCI and data-visualisation thread I continued as Research Assistant II (VR) at Hong Kong Metropolitan University.

Focus: Unity 3D VR, scenario scripting, traffic logic, and gaze-based interaction design.

2022 Nov - 2023 Nov
Spatial Project

A research-oriented Unity mobile suite of five mini-games used to measure and train spatial ability. Each title targets different cognitive and motor patterns; together they support experiments on reaction time, accuracy, and learning curves across sessions.

As lead developer, I owned end-to-end delivery: C# gameplay and UI/UX, narrative and level design, art direction for scenes, and a backend database for secure login, session logging, and research datasets—parallel to the “login and database systems for game data and user research” work described in my Research Assistant II role at The University of Hong Kong.

Focus: Unity UI/UX, experimental game design, data-backed HCI research, and full-stack integration between clients and storage.

2023 Apr - 2024 Apr
Smart Traffic Fund - VR Driving School Simulator

The funded evolution of the VR Driving School line: I served as Project Manager for the Smart Traffic Fund’s “Virtual Reality-based Driving Training System,” extending earlier eye-tracking work with a full hand-tracking stack so trainees can operate the cabin believably—hand brake, gear lever, hazards, indicators, start button, and related interior controls—while analytics still support HCI and performance review.

Outdoor Hong Kong context is delivered through a 3D reconstruction / Gaussian splat pipeline for street-level realism, complementing my skills in point-cloud capture (e.g. LiDAR) and processing in tools such as Reality Capture for believable environment authoring.

Outreach: I organised and ran multiple public and partner demonstrations—including showcases at Lee Kin Driving School and HKUST—so industry and academia could trial the simulator and give structured feedback.

2023 May - 2023 July
3D TPS Mobile Game (Whoii World)

A Unity-based third-person 3D mobile title built around hide-and-seek and light combat across varied environments. The goal was a polished handheld experience with readable UI, stable frame rates on mid-tier phones, and strong scene readability for multiplayer-style cat-and-mouse play.

I contributed across the production stack: UI/UX flows and HUD, scene optimisation and occlusion-friendly layouts, core C# gameplay for the hide-and-seek loop, post-processing and lighting passes, narrative beats, and Cinemachine-driven cinematics for NPC cutscenes—matching the kind of end-to-end Unity 3D work highlighted in my HKU mobile-game research period.

Focus: Mobile Unity pipelines, VFX/UI balance, systems design, and scripted sequences for player onboarding and story moments.

Dec 2025
華富邨 · Wah Fu Estate

A multi-page, bilingual-friendly web experience (Traditional Chinese, Hong Kong) that introduces Wah Fu Estate—a landmark public housing community—as a living neighbourhood, not only a building complex. The site mirrors how I approach multimedia teaching: clear information architecture, strong visual narrative, and interactive elements that invite exploration rather than passive reading.

The build follows a classic content site structure: a landing hero and overview, an interactive history timeline (1920s–1980s), a Spot section with a Leaflet.js map plus practical visitor information, long-form Story pages on people and places, and a Future area with before/after treatments and redevelopment context. Shared CSS and JavaScript tie the sections together for consistent UX and light interactivity (carousels, map, timeline behaviour).

Stack: semantic HTML, modular stylesheets, client-side scripting, and optional Node/Express hosting for delivery—overlapping with foundational web topics and digital publishing skills relevant to higher-diploma IT and multimedia pathways.

Focus: Community heritage, editorial UX, interactive maps, timelines, and responsive layout for cultural storytelling on the open web.

Apr 2025
The Architecture of Illusion

Interactive web report on synthetic media

Role: UX Engineer · Frontend Developer · Content Designer

A multi-chapter, dark-themed interactive web documentary that unpacks how AI deepfakes and voice cloning work, where they help or harm society, and how audiences can reason about synthetic media. The experience is structured as a guided editorial journey—hero video, manifesto, key statistics with citations, and clearly signposted sections on history, mechanics, benefits, threats, myths, prevention, and references—so complex material reads like a premium long-form report rather than a static PDF.

I led UX, front-end implementation, and content design: information hierarchy, motion and contrast for readability, and turning machine-learning ideas (e.g. GANs, autoencoders, zero-shot voice cloning) into layered explanations, custom SVG schematics, and a Chart.js visualisation for quantitative storytelling. The interface leans on HTML5, CSS Grid, custom properties, fluid typography (clamp()), glass-style surfaces, and gradient systems for a cohesive immersive feel without a framework.

Interaction layer: bespoke vanilla JavaScript components—steppers, accordions, and tabbed panels—to pace disclosure and keep readers oriented inside long pages. The work sits alongside my AI & digital media background: rigorous citing, plain-language framing, and design choices that support media literacy rather than hype.

Stack: HTML5, advanced CSS3 (Grid, variables, fluid type, gradients, glassmorphism), Vanilla JS, Chart.js · Focus: Editorial UX, data storytelling, and accessible explanations of generative AI on the open web.
